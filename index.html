Research 研究

RS-01 · published · 2026

Digital Learning for Religious Moderation: Assessing the Impact of MOOCs on Understanding Wasathiyah Islam 数字化学习与宗教温和：MOOC对理解中道伊斯兰影响的评估

Examines whether MOOCs can improve students' understanding of Wasathiyah Islam (moderate, balanced Islam). Using a quantitative pretest–posttest design, understanding scores rose significantly (Wilcoxon Z = -4.675, p < 0.001), confirming MOOCs as an effective tool for strengthening religious moderation.

本研究探讨MOOC（大规模开放在线课程）能否提升学生对"中道伊斯兰"（Wasathiyah Islam，即温和平衡的伊斯兰理念）的理解。采用定量前测-后测设计，学生理解程度显著提升（Wilcoxon检验 Z = -4.675，p < 0.001），证实MOOC是强化宗教温和的有效工具。

RS-02 · published · 2026

Assessing the Level of AI Literacy among Muslim University Students: A Rasch Model Analysis 评估穆斯林大学生的人工智能素养水平：基于Rasch模型的分析

Investigates AI literacy among 286 Muslim university students in Indonesia using Rasch model analysis (Winsteps). Findings show generally moderate-to-high AI literacy and an adaptive attitude toward AI technology, viewed through an Islamic ethical lens (itqan and amanah).

本研究运用Rasch模型分析（Winsteps），调查286名印尼穆斯林大学生的人工智能素养水平。结果显示学生整体AI素养处于中高水平，并对AI技术持适应态度，研究同时结合伊斯兰伦理观（精益求精 itqan 与诚信 amanah）进行审视。

RS-03 · published · 2024

Unity in Diversity: Examining Religious Moderation across Religions in Indonesia 多元一体：印尼跨宗教宗教温和现象考察

A literature review examining religious moderation across Indonesia's six recognized religions plus local indigenous beliefs. Finds that while expressions of moderation vary by faith community, interfaith initiatives, religious institutions, and education are key to sustaining Indonesia's "Unity in Diversity."

本研究通过文献综述，考察印尼六大官方认可宗教及本土信仰中宗教温和的不同表现。研究发现，尽管各信仰群体的温和实践各异，跨宗教倡议、宗教机构与教育仍是维系印尼"多元一体"精神的关键因素。
